Original draft (do not edit)

As the annual THON event draws near, thousands of students are getting ready to spend the entirety of their weekends in the Bryce Jordan Center to dance in hopes of a cure for pediatric cancer. For THON, the focus is not just on dancing and fundraising, but food is also a crucial element to the weekend. While dancers and attendees push their physical limits for 46 hours, many students find themselves struggling with a lack of nutritious food options and limited access to meals that combat their dietary restrictions.

For the volunteers and dancers at THON, food is not just about fueling up; it is necessary to stay healthy, energized, and be able to conquer the weekend. However, many attendees find themselves in a pinch with the lack of options that the BJC has to offer. Foods fail to align with their dietary preferences and the limited healthy alternatives sell out early in the weekend. Food accessibility remains a key concern for those spending long hours on their feet.

Valerie DeSantos, a seasoned THON participant, highlighted one of the biggest issues: "The BJC doesn't have enough healthy options, and what they do have often sells out quickly." She specifically mentioned fruit and breakfast sandwiches as popular items that are typically unavailable later into the weekend, which leaves those with dietary restrictions having to settle for less desired options like fried foods.
